Slide 1: Introduction

Cybersecurity is more critical than ever. Hackers use multiple methods to hijack accounts, steal data, or spread scams.

This guide will teach you how to spot hacking attempts, protect your devices, and recover if hacked.

Slide 2: Common Hacking Methods

  • Phishing: Fake emails or texts trick users into entering login details.
  • Smishing: Fraudulent texts claim to be delivery notifications, job offers, or bank alerts.
  • Malware & Spyware: Hidden apps record keystrokes, steal data, or spy on activity.
  • SIM Swapping: Attackers convince mobile carriers to transfer your number to their SIM.
  • Public Wi-Fi Attacks: Hackers intercept data from unsecured networks.
  • Evil Twin Attack: Fake Wi-Fi hotspots steal logins, passwords, and financial details.
  • Bluetooth Hacking: Leaving Bluetooth enabled in public lets hackers connect remotely.
  • Fake Apps: Malware hidden in fake versions of popular apps.
  • Juice Jacking: Malicious charging stations install malware onto devices.
  • Rogue QR Codes: Scammers replace QR codes with fraudulent ones.

Slide 3: Real-World Examples of Hacking

Example 1: Bank Phishing Scam

John gets a fake SMS saying his bank account is locked. He clicks a fraudulent link, enters his credentials, and loses money.

Example 2: Malware from Fake Apps

Sarah installs a free weather app from an unverified source. The app secretly collects texts, contacts, and login credentials.

Example 3: Public Wi-Fi Interception

Tom connects to free Wi-Fi at a coffee shop. He unknowingly connects to a hacker-controlled network, exposing his email login.

Example 4: Discord Token Theft

Emma receives a DM about a "free Nitro giveaway." She enters her login details into a fraudulent webpage, and the hacker steals her Discord token.

Slide 4: Signs You’ve Been Hacked

  • Phone Signs: Battery drains fast, unusual data spikes, strange apps appear.
  • Computer Signs: Frequent crashes or slowdowns, unwanted browser extensions, homepage changes.
  • Social Media Signs: Unwanted posts, friends receive scam messages, password no longer works.

Slide 5: How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong passwords (never reuse them).
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts.
  • Regularly update apps and security patches.
  • Limit app permissions (only allow necessary access).
  • Use a VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi.

Slide 6: What to Do If You’re Hacked

  • Change All Passwords Immediately: Use strong, unique passwords and never reuse old ones.
  • Log Out of All Devices: Force logout from Google, Microsoft, Discord, and social media accounts.
  • Scan Your Phone & PC for Malware: Use antivirus software like Malwarebytes or Windows Defender.
  • Check Your Bank Accounts for Suspicious Activity: Freeze payments if you detect unauthorized transactions.
  • Warn Friends About Possible Phishing Messages: If hackers used your account to send scam links.
  • Factory Reset Your Phone: If necessary, back up your data and erase everything.
  • Report the Hack to Cybersecurity Authorities: Contact CISA (US), Europol (EU), or your local cybersecurity agency.

Slide 7: Protecting Your Discord & Social Media Accounts

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Protects against unauthorized logins.
  • Use a Strong Password: Avoid common phrases and reuse across platforms.
  • Monitor Login History: Regularly check account activity on Discord, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter/X, and TikTok.
  • Ignore Suspicious Links & Messages: Never click random links in DMs or emails.
  • Disable Third-Party Connections: Remove access to unknown apps linked to your accounts.
  • Review Security Settings: Check Discord’s "Active Sessions" tab to remove unwanted logins.

Slide 8: Signs Your Social Media & Messaging Apps Are Hacked

  • Unrecognized logins from unknown locations.
  • Friends receiving spam messages or scam links from your account.
  • Password no longer works.
  • Unwanted ads or posts appear on your profile.
  • New followers or accounts linked that you didn’t add.
  • Your profile picture or display name changes unexpectedly.
  • Strange call logs or messages auto-forwarding.

Slide 9: How to Secure Your Messaging Apps (WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal)

  • Use Encrypted Backup Settings: Protects chat history from unauthorized access.
  • Disable Automatic Downloads: Prevent malware-infected media from downloading automatically.
  • Enable Disappearing Messages: Reduces exposure of long-term stored data.
  • Monitor Call Logs & Message History: Check for unfamiliar numbers or forwarded messages.

Slide 10: Final Cybersecurity Tips & Best Practices

  • Keep all software updated.
  • Use a password manager for stronger security.
  • Enable biometric authentication where possible.
  • Use a VPN to encrypt sensitive connections.
  • Stay informed about the latest cybersecurity threats.
