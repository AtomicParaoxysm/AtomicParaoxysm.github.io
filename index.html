üß† Common Hacking Methods (with Examples)

Phishing: Fake emails mimic real services to steal login credentials.
Smishing: Fake SMS messages trick you into clicking malicious links.
Malware & Spyware: Harmful apps secretly monitor your activity or steal information.
SIM Swapping: An attacker transfers your phone number to their SIM to intercept 2FA codes.
Public Wi-Fi Attacks: Hackers eavesdrop on unsecured public networks to capture data.
Evil Twin Networks: Lookalike Wi-Fi networks lure users into unsafe connections.
Bluetooth Hacking: Hackers exploit open Bluetooth connections to access devices.
Fake Apps: Apps designed to look legit but steal data or install malware.
Juice Jacking: Charging via public USB ports that transmit malware.
Rogue QR Codes: Scanning redirects you to malicious websites or downloads.
Social Engineering: Psychological manipulation that tricks you into revealing confidential info.
Credential Stuffing: Reusing breached passwords to access other accounts.
Man-in-the-Middle (MITM): Attackers intercept communications between you and a website.
Fake Support Scams: Calls or popups pretending to be tech support to gain access.
Social Media/Discord Hacking: Token theft through fake links spreads scams via compromised accounts.

üÜò What to Do If You‚Äôre Hacked

  • Change passwords immediately, especially email, banking, and recovery accounts.
  • Run malware and virus scans using trusted security software.
