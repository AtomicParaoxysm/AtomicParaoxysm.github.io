Understanding How Hacking Happens & How to Stay Safe

Why cybersecurity matters for phone users

Growing threats of mobile hacking and data theft

Slide 2: Common Hacking Methods

  • Phishing: Fake emails trick users into revealing data.
  • Malware: Harmful apps steal information.
  • SIM Swapping: Attackers hijack phone numbers.
  • Public Wi-Fi Attacks: Hackers intercept data.
  • Juice Jacking: Malicious charging stations infect devices.

Slide 3: Examples of Hacking

Phishing Scam: Fake banking websites steal login credentials.

Malware Attack: Malicious apps secretly install spyware.

Public Wi-Fi Interception: Hackers steal data from open networks.

Slide 4: Signs Your Phone Might Be Hacked

  • Battery drains unusually fast
  • Unexpected pop-ups or ads
  • Unauthorized transactions or logins
  • Slow performance or overheating

Slide 5: How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Keep software updated to patch security vulnerabilities.
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown apps.
  • Use a VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi.

Slide 6: What to Do If You Get Hacked

  • Reset passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Scan your phone for malware.
  • Monitor financial accounts for unauthorized transactions.
  • Perform a factory reset if necessary.

Slide 7: Protecting Your Discord Account

Common threats: Token theft, phishing, and credential stuffing.

Safety Tips: Enable 2FA, use strong passwords, and avoid clicking unknown links.
